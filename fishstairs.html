THE FISH

STAIRS

a painted stairway that transforms everyday urban
infrastructure into a vibrant work of public art.
Located on Arany János street, the staircase leads
down to Tapolca’s picturesque Malom (Mill) Lake,
seamlessly connecting the town’s natural setting
with its creative spirit

In 2016 Judit Varjas worked from dawn until
night for 16 days and used 30 kilograms of
paint to decorate the 27 steps. This work of art
was refreshed in the autumn of 2022. The
repair work took twenty-two 10-12-hour days,
made difficult by bad weather. The result,
however, speaks for itself.

2016
2025

KOI FISH

The design centers on koi fish symbolic residents
of the nearby lake, swimming through waves of
vibrant blues and teals that ripple across the risers.
This aquatic theme blurs the line between the local
environment and the art itself: as visitors ascend or
descend the stairs, they feel almost as if they are
moving through water, guided by schools of fish.

DID YOU KNOW?

This attraction was inspired by the stairs in Valparaíso, Chile.
